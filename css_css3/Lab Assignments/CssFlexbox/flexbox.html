Article 370 of the Constitution of India granted Jammu and Kashmir special status, including the ability to create its own constitution and flag, and to govern itself internally:

  • Autonomy: Article 370 allowed Jammu and Kashmir to make its own laws in most areas, except for defense, finance, communications, and foreign affairs.
  • Separate constitution: Jammu and Kashmir was allowed to create its own constitution.
  • State flag: Jammu and Kashmir was allowed to create its own flag.
  • Internal administration: Jammu and Kashmir was allowed to govern itself internally.
  • Property privileges: Outsiders were denied property privileges in Jammu and Kashmir.

Article 370: What is Article 370, Article 370 News, Article ... Article 370 was introduced to the Constituent Assembly in 1949 and was intended to be a temporary provision. It was intended to provide a framework for negotiations between the Indian government and Jammu and Kashmir's leadership, and to determine the state's final political status.

Article 370 was extended to Jammu and Kashmir in 1954 through a Presidential Order. However, between 1954 and 2011, the Indian government used Article 370 to issue presidential orders that reduced the state's autonomy.
